About us

The Alzheimer’s Prevention project aims to raise the awareness on Alzheimer’s disease to people who are in their midlife, age between 40 to 60 years old. Studies show that chance of Alzheimer’s development is highset at 65 and death because of Alzheimer’s disease has increased over years in Australia.

Therefore, with considering decline on body fitness level after 40, staying physically and mentally active and follow healthy diet as well as understanding and controlling modifiable risk factors such as obesity and physical activity would be beneficial not only in Alzheimer’s prevention but also it increases the fitness level.

The Alzheimer’s prevention website includes important information regarding to disease risk factors and providing comprehensive source of information that users can use and reach out to learn more.

The focus of this website is on obesity and physical inactivity among the list of risk factors and encourage healthy people age between 40 to 60 to understand the risks and probability of disease development in future based on their status of BMI (Body Mass Index) and weekly physical activity.

In addition, it enhances users’ act of prevention by providing list of relevant exercises and activities both physically and mentally where they can easily access to all information they needed under their fingerprints.

Users also are able to find nearest location based on their choice of sport and activity on the map.

Other information regarding to proper diet for Alzheimer’s prevention are included as well.

It is necessary to mention that all information, suggestions and calculation are based on academic research and added as references and related documents section on website and webpages.
